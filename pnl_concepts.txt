DOCUMENT: PnL Anomaly Types and Causes
SOURCE: Internal Finance Reference Guide

--- LARGE LOSS SPIKE ---
A large loss spike occurs when a single trade or position shows a loss significantly larger than the desk's historical daily average.
Common causes:
- Incorrect trade booking (wrong notional, wrong side of trade)
- Market data feed error (stale or erroneous price used for mark-to-market)
- Unauthorized position taken outside approved limits
- Genuine adverse market move (e.g., flash crash, unexpected rate hike)
Recommended action: Immediately verify the trade booking details against the original order ticket. Check market data timestamps. If booking looks correct, escalate to risk management.

--- SUDDEN PnL REVERSAL ---
A sudden PnL reversal is when a position that was showing significant gains flips to losses (or vice versa) within a single day without a corresponding market event.
Common causes:
- Trade amendment or cancellation processed late
- Valuation model parameter update (e.g., volatility surface refresh)
- Incorrect accrual or coupon payment posted
Recommended action: Review any trade amendments submitted in the past 24 hours. Verify model parameters match the official market data snapshot.

--- ZERO PnL ON ACTIVE POSITION ---
A position that should be generating daily PnL shows exactly zero. This is statistically very unlikely for any live market position.
Common causes:
- Position accidentally marked as inactive in the system
- Market data feed disconnection causing stale prices
- Reconciliation error where offsetting trades netted incorrectly
Recommended action: Confirm position status in the trade management system. Verify market data feed connectivity.

--- OUTLIER COMPARED TO DESK AVERAGE ---
A trade whose daily PnL deviates more than 3 standard deviations from the desk's mean is flagged as a statistical outlier.
Common causes:
- Unusual concentration of risk in a single name or sector
- Structured product with non-linear payoff triggered
- Erroneous multiplier applied to notional
Recommended action: Review position sizing and concentration limits. Confirm notional and multiplier fields in the trade booking system.

DOCUMENT: Mark-to-Market Valuation Basics
SOURCE: Risk Management Primer

Mark-to-market (MTM) is the practice of valuing a position at its current market price rather than its historical cost.
Daily PnL = (Today's MTM Price - Yesterday's MTM Price) x Notional
For derivatives, the MTM calculation uses pricing models (Black-Scholes for options, discounted cash flow for swaps).
A booking error in notional has a linear effect on PnL — doubling the notional doubles the PnL impact.
A model parameter error (e.g., wrong volatility) has a non-linear effect for options positions.

DOCUMENT: Trade Reconciliation Process
SOURCE: Operations Manual

Reconciliation is the process of matching internal trade records against external counterparty confirmations and exchange records.
Breaks in reconciliation (unmatched trades) are a leading indicator of booking errors.
A "long break" (open for more than 2 business days) requires escalation to the trade support desk.
Reconciliation differences in PnL that exceed $10,000 per trade must be reported to the desk head the same day.
Common reconciliation error types:
1. Quantity mismatch: internal system shows different notional than counterparty confirms
2. Price mismatch: trade booked at wrong execution price
3. Date mismatch: settlement date entered incorrectly, causing accrual timing errors
